Can Computers Learn Common Sense?


https://www.newyorker.com/tech/annals-of-technology/can-computers-learn-common-sense
April 5, 2022

A computer scientist named Yejin Choi says common sense is the “dark matter” of Artificial Intelligence. “It shapes so much of what we do and what we need to do, and yet its ineffable.” She states that computers are puzzled by problems called “cheeseburger stabbing.” This piece gives a nice overview of efforts to build that dark matter into modern algorithms, and the sometimes fraught role humans play in doing so.

How researchers trained a budget robot dog to do tricks

https://www.popsci.com/technology/parkour-algorithm-robodog/ OCT 5, 2023

Robot dogs are often trained using a mix of techniques such as reinforcement learning and imitation learning. Reinforcement learning is the process by which a robot learns via trial and error, receiving positive reinforcement for right acts and adjusting its behavior accordingly. In contrast, imitation learning includes the robot watching and emulating human or animal actions in order to complete specified tasks.

How factories are deploying AI on production lines

https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-67144713 NOV 15, 2023

PepsiCo is deploying Augury-powered artificial intelligence sensors across its factories, aiming to increase efficiency, reduce waste, and expedite product delivery. These sensors, trained to recognize signs of weary machines, are being deployed across the factory following a successful US trial. The technology can analyze processes in real-time, warn of future issues, and use historical data to recommend fixes.
